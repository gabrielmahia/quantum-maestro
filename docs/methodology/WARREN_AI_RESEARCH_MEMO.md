## 1. WHAT THE RESEARCH FOUND — TERI IJEOMA (IWT)

### What is actually taught (vs. the headline)

Teri's headline is "make 1% per day." The real system is more nuanced and disciplined:

**Seven steps, in exact order:**
1. **Company quality first.** Teri trades the same ~30 liquid tech stocks she has studied for
   14+ years. Not random tickers. Not penny stocks. Ever.
2. **Stop loss before entry.** Her first major loss (Pandora) was held. She learned.
   The stop is the system. Everything else is commentary.
3. **Technical fluency.** Gaps, Globex levels, support/resistance, volume confirmation.
   Stocks 101 curriculum updated 2025 to include gap trading and Globex methodology.
4. **Entry only at support.** Never more than 2% from the level. If you missed it: WAIT.
5. **1% daily goal.** Creates discipline ceiling. Prevents greed from compounding losses.
6. **Options as amplifiers.** CSPs → covered calls → DITM long calls, in that order.
   Cost basis reduction over time is the IWT compounding engine.
7. **Track everything.** Journal every trade. Troubleshoot with the 7-symptom checklist.

**New in 2025-2026:**
- Gap trading added to core curriculum (gap types, Globex levels)
- Book "The Risk Worth Taking" published April 2026
- Coaching note: 2020 recordings relevant for tariff environment — conditions rhyme with 2025

**What IWT gets right:**
- Discipline over prediction
- Defined risk before entry (structural, not arbitrary)
- Options used for income, not gambling
- Two-account system (scout vs. production) prevents emotional live trading

**What IWT doesn't address:**
- Regime context (when to stop entirely, not just size down)
- Macro filters (rates, credit spreads, dollar)
- Breadth analysis (whether the market supports any bullish setups)
- Stage analysis (buying at support in a Stage 4 stock is still wrong)

## 2. WHAT THE RESEARCH FOUND — RAY DALIO (All Weather)

### The insight that survives, and the allocation that doesn't

**The insight (SURVIVES):**
Four economic regimes exist: rising growth/inflation, falling growth/inflation (4 combinations).
No one can predict which regime is next. Build portfolios that perform reasonably in all four.
Balance RISK, not capital, across asset classes.

**The classic allocation (PARTIALLY OUTDATED for 2026):**
30% stocks, 40% long bonds, 15% intermediate bonds, 7.5% gold, 7.5% commodities

**The 2022 failure (CONFIRMED, -18% drawdown):**
The All Weather portfolio was implicitly calibrated to the 1980-2020 "Great Disinflation" —
40 years of falling rates during which bonds provided both income AND capital gains.
When rates spiked in 2022, the 55% bond weight became a liability.
All Weather lost ~18% in 2022 — its worst year ever — in the exact conditions it was
designed to protect against (rising inflation / falling growth = Dalio's Regime 2).

**The updated application:**
- Dalio's REGIME FRAMEWORK is more valuable than his specific allocation
- In a 4-5% rate environment: reduce long bond duration, increase short-duration and TIPS
- Bridgewater itself launched ALLW ETF (March 2025) using 1.8× leverage via futures — the
  underlying conviction in risk-parity remains, but implementation evolves
- For traders: use the four regimes as a CONTEXT filter, not a portfolio allocation formula

**Key Dalio quotes for traders:**
- "5 good uncorrelated return streams, risk balanced... I can lower risk by up to 80%"
- "I won't lower return, but I can lower risk dramatically through diversification"
- "We're in a changing world order" (debt, dollar, geopolitics) — act accordingly

**What this tells traders:**

$13B/year in T-bills, the opportunity cost of deploying capital is very high.
This is not pessimism — it is precision. He is not finding sufficient margin of safety.

**The circle of competence principle — applied to options:**
Never sell a cash-secured put on a company you wouldn't want to own.
If you don't want to own 100 shares of a company at your strike price, you should not
be selling the put. This is the Buffett filter for IWT CSP trading.

**Economic moat applied to watchlist construction:**
Before any ticker enters your IWT watchlist, ask: Does this company have a structural
competitive advantage? Coca-Cola's moat: brand + distribution. Apple's moat: ecosystem
lock-in. Without a moat, the company is a trade, not an investment anchor.

**Buffett's holding principle creates IWT patience framework:**
"My favorite holding period is forever." For a trader, the parallel is:
"My favorite support is one I've watched hold multiple times over multiple months."
Familiarity with how a stock behaves at support IS a form of circle of competence.

## 4. WHAT THE RESEARCH FOUND — MARK MINERVINI

### The framework IWT was missing

**Why Minervini matters for IWT traders:**
IWT says "enter at support." Minervini says "only enter at support if the stock is in Stage 2."
These are not contradictory — they are sequential. Minervini's stage filter prevents
the most common IWT mistake: buying a support level in a Stage 4 declining stock.

**The Trend Template (8-point filter):**
All 8 criteria act as a pre-qualification. If the stock fails even one, it is not
a candidate, period. The template prevents entering stocks that "look cheap" but are
actually in downtrends where each bounce leads to lower lows.

**VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) + IWT support = optimal entry:**
A VCP base that forms AT an IWT support level, with the VCP's final contraction at
precisely that support, is the highest-quality entry confluence in this system.
Volume drying up at support = sellers exhausted. Breakout on volume = buyers committed.
This is the IWT setup plus Minervini's timing precision.

**Stage analysis prevents the "falling knife" error:**
A Stage 4 stock (declining, below all MAs, MAs trending down) may appear to have
"great support levels" on the chart. Those levels get violated. The stock is in a
downtrend. No amount of support analysis changes that.
Stage 2 only. This is the guard rail IWT doesn't explicitly provide.

**Minervini stop logic (7-8% normal, 5-6% weak market):**
This aligns with the IWT 0.5× ATR stop. The tightening in weak markets is the key
insight: when the overall market is weak (YELLOW/RED regime), stops should be tighter,
because failed setups will fail faster and more decisively.

These are completely separate strategies requiring separate capital allocation and
risk management. 0DTE can generate 21% in a day and lose 100% in a day.
Never mix 0DTE sizing/risk logic with 30-45 DTE position management.

## 6. HOW THE FRAMEWORKS LAYER

**The decision cascade:**

Step 1 — Dalio regime check:
→ 🟢 GREEN: Proceed
→ 🟡 YELLOW: Reduce size 50%, only cleanest setups
→ 🔴 RED: Cash only. Close short premium.

Step 2 — Buffett quality filter:
→ Is this a quality company with a moat?
→ Would I own this if I had to hold it for a year?
→ Is it within my circle of competence?
If no: SKIP. Go to next ticker.

Step 3 — Minervini stage filter:
→ Is the stock in Stage 2?
→ Does it pass the Trend Template (6+ of 8)?
→ Is there a VCP or identifiable base to enter from?
If Stage 1/3/4: SKIP.

Step 4 — IWT entry execution:
→ Is price at support (within 2%)?
→ Has stop been defined?
→ Is R:R ≥ 2:1?
→ Which IWT options structure fits the regime?
→ Execute.

**When all four agree:** HIGH conviction. Standard size.
**When three agree:** MEDIUM conviction. 75% size.
**When two agree:** LOW conviction. 50% size or WAIT.
**When they conflict:** DO NOT TRADE. Document. Revisit.
