# LAYER 2 — BUFFETT QUALITY FILTER

*Source: Berkshire Hathaway letters 1977-2026. $373B cash position as of Q1 2026 — the largest in history.*

**Apply this filter BEFORE entering any position on a company.**

A quality company must clear all six tests:

1. **Economic moat:** Is the competitive advantage structural (brand, network effect, switching costs, cost scale, regulatory protection)? Not "strong last quarter" — durable over 10+ years.

2. **Circle of competence:** Do you understand how this business makes money? If not: DO NOT TRADE. Buffett avoided tech for decades, not because tech was bad, but because he couldn't predict 10-year winners. He eventually bought Apple after studying consumer behavior for years.

3. **Intrinsic value margin of safety:** Buy at 70% or less of intrinsic value. For options: enter when the stock is at or below the IWT support level, not chasing. Buffett's $373B cash in 2026 exists because he found *insufficient margin of safety* in most US equities. His war chest is patience made visible.

4. **Management quality:** Does management allocate capital wisely? Do they eat their own cooking (insider ownership)?

5. **Earnings consistency:** ≥ 5 years of consistent EPS growth, ROE > 15%, low net debt. No one-hit wonders.

6. **Cash flow over earnings:** A company printing cash flow is real. Accounting earnings can be managed. Free cash flow cannot.

**Current Buffett signal (July 2026):** Net seller for 12+ consecutive quarters. $373B cash generating $13B/year risk-free at T-bill rates. Reading: "Either stocks are overvalued, or I'm being paid to wait." Either scenario is informative.

**For options traders:** Buffett quality filter applies to underlying selection. Never sell a cash-secured put on a company you wouldn't want to own. If you don't want to own 100 shares of the company at your strike, don't sell the put.

# LAYER 3 — MINERVINI STAGE ANALYSIS + TREND TEMPLATE

*Source: SEPA methodology. US Investing Champion 1997 (155% audited), 2021 (334.8% audited). Stage analysis adapted from Stan Weinstein.*

**Only buy (or sell puts on) Stage 2 stocks. No exceptions.**

**Four Stages:**
- **Stage 1 — Basing:** Sideways after decline. MAs flat. Volume dull. WAIT.
- **Stage 2 — Advancing:** Breakout from Stage 1. Rising 50, 150, 200 MA. Higher highs, higher lows. 50 > 150 > 200. THIS IS THE ONLY BUY ZONE.
- **Stage 3 — Topping:** Advance stalls. Pullbacks deepen. Rallies fail. SELL OR AVOID.
- **Stage 4 — Declining:** Breakdown below MAs. MAs trending down. DO NOT BUY DIPS.

**Minervini Trend Template (8-point checklist — ALL required):**
1. Stock price above 150-day and 200-day SMA
2. 150-day SMA above 200-day SMA
3. 200-day SMA trending UP for at least 1 month (4-5 months preferred)
4. 50-day SMA above both 150-day and 200-day SMA
5. Stock price at least 30% above 52-week low
6. Stock price within 25% of 52-week high
7. Relative strength (RS) score ≥ 85 (outperforms 85% of market)
8. Institutional buying visible in volume patterns

**VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):**
The ideal entry is a breakout from a VCP — a base where each successive pullback is smaller (e.g., 15% → 10% → 6% → 3%) with declining volume on each pullback, then a breakout on expanding volume. The contracting volatility signals supply exhaustion. The volume surge on breakout signals institutional demand overwhelming supply.

**Stop loss rules (Minervini):**
- Normal market: 7-8% below pivot
- Weak/volatile market: tighten to 5-6%
- Maximum position concentration: 4-8 positions. NOT 20-30.

**Key Minervini insight for options traders:** VCP breakouts are also ideal CSP (cash-secured put) setups — sell OTM puts at or below the base support level BEFORE the breakout, collect premium as the VCP resolves, assignment unlikely if pattern is valid.

# LAYER 4 — TERI IJEOMA IWT METHOD (Complete 7-Step Framework)

*Source: Invest With Teri (IWT), Trade and Travel Stocks 101 + VIP curriculum, 2020-2026 coaching calls. Teri's book "The Risk Worth Taking" (April 2026).*

**The IWT 7-Step Investing Method:**

### Step 1: Pick Good Companies
Not penny stocks. Never penny stocks — too volatile, too manipulable.  
Teri's universe: ~30 liquid, well-known tech companies she has traded for 14+ years.  
Quality test: Companies you've heard of, that have real products, real earnings.  
Minimum: Listed, liquid, optionable, recognizable brand.

### Step 2: Manage Risk (Stop Loss Always)
**Structure the stop BEFORE entering. Never skip this.**  
Stop placement: Below structural support (Lesson 2 + Minervini combined).  
IWT formula: 0.5× ATR below the most recent swing low support.  
If the stock gaps below your stop: EXIT. Do not hope, do not average down.  
Teri's first major loss (Pandora) taught her this. She kept it. She lost.  
After loss: she rebuilt around discipline. The stop loss is the system.

### Step 3: Read Charts (Technical Fluency)
- Identify support and resistance levels
- Recognize chart patterns: cup and handle, flag, wedge, gap fill
- Volume confirms price: rising price + rising volume = institutional accumulation
- Gap types matter: Common, Breakaway, Runaway, Exhaustion — each has different trading implications
- Globex (overnight/pre-market) levels: Teri tracks Globex highs and lows as key intraday reference levels
- Late entry detection: If price is >2% above the support/entry level, the entry is late. WAIT for the next setup.

### Step 4: Enter at Support
This is the core IWT discipline.  
**Buy at support. NEVER chase.**  
Support = a price level where buyers have previously stepped in, visible on chart as a horizontal zone.  
Entry zone: Within 0.5-1% of the support level.  
Confirmation: Volume should be elevated at support (buyers showing up).  
IWT watchlist approach: Teri watches the same ~30 stocks for years. She knows their support levels. Familiarity creates edge.

### Step 5: Set 1% Daily Goal
**Goal: Make 1% per day on deployed capital.**  
$10,000 account → $100/day goal  
$50,000 account → $500/day goal  
The 1% goal prevents overtrading and greed.  
Daily stop-trading rule: If you hit 2× the daily goal, consider stopping for the day.  
If you lose 2× the daily goal, STOP TRADING. Reset tomorrow.

### Step 6: Use Options for Premium (Odds Enhancers)
After learning stocks, add options for leverage and income.  
IWT options hierarchy:
1. **Cash-secured puts (CSP):** Sell to buy stock cheaper or collect premium outright. Preferred in sideways/mildly bullish markets at support.
2. **Covered calls:** After owning stock at support, sell calls above resistance for income. Reduces cost basis.
3. **Long calls (DITM, 60+ DTE):** When trend is strong and breakout confirmed. Buy calls with >0.70 delta, at least 60 DTE. Pays more than stock on large moves with defined risk.
4. **Vertical spreads:** When directional but want defined risk both ways.
5. **Never:** Naked options, earnings plays with premium selling, options <7 DTE (unless expert).

**Covered call yield formula (Teri's favorite):**
Premium collected ÷ Stock price × (365 ÷ DTE) = Annualized yield  
Target: ≥ 12-18% annualized yield on covered calls.

**Cost basis reduction (IWT cornerstone):**
Each covered call sold reduces the effective purchase price of the stock.  
After selling enough covered calls, the "house's money" principle kicks in — the stock was effectively acquired for free.

### Step 7: Track, Review, Troubleshoot
- Log EVERY trade: entry, stop, target, actual exit, P&L, what you learned
- Review weekly: Are wins and losses within expected ranges?
- Troubleshoot checklist (Teri's 7-symptom diagnostic):
  1. Entering too late (>2% from support)?
  2. Stop too wide (>8% from entry)?
  3. Position too large (>2% of account per trade for beginners)?
  4. Trading earnings blindly?
  5. Trading unfamiliar tickers?
  6. Chasing after missing the entry?
  7. Ignoring the market regime?

**IWT Two-Account Philosophy:**
- Account A (Scout): Paper or very small size. Test the setup. Confirm pattern. Build the data.
- Account B (Production): Real capital. Only deploy after Account A confirms 30+ trades of the setup working.

**IWT Gap Trading (added to Stocks 101 curriculum 2025):**
Gap types and strategies:
- **Common gap:** Small gap, often fills within days. Fade the gap.
- **Breakaway gap:** Large gap on volume from a base. Do NOT fade. Trade with trend.
- **Runaway gap:** Mid-trend continuation. Add or ride.
- **Exhaustion gap:** Near extended move. DANGER. Do not chase.
Globex levels: Overnight futures activity sets reference. If price gaps above globex high on open, watch for early momentum. If it fades back through, reversal likely.

# BEHAVIORAL GUARDRAILS

These rules exist because emotions destroy systematic traders.
